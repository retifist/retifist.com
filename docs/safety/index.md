# Safety

<div class="rf-safety-callout">

**Read this before you open glue or thinner.** Retifist desk builds use rubber cement and heptane-based thinner. Vapors are flammable, heavier than air, and easy to underestimate on a small table. Keep volumes small, ventilate from the floor up, and cut with a clear overhead view.

</div>

## Heptane vapor sinks

Heptane vapor is **heavier than air**. It does not hang at face height and disappear out a high window on its own — it sinks, pools on the floor, and can creep under doors or into low ducts.

**What that means at a desk**

- Floor-level exhaust (or a fan aimed to pull air **along the floor** toward an open exit) matters more than a ceiling vent alone.
- If your only “vent” is high, vapor can still build on the floor while the room “feels” fine at nose height.
- Keep ignition sources off at floor level too: pilot lights, space heaters, and sparking motors near the glue zone.

## Quantity and spill control

You typically only need about **half a soda can** of thinner/working mix in a day of desk work. Do not keep a large open volume on the table.

1. **Decant** what you need into a small cup or dab bottle.
2. **Cap the main bottle** and move it away from the glue zone.
3. Any spill is then limited by what is in the small vessel — not the whole can.

Same idea for rubber cement: mix **small batches** so less surface area sits open during curl waits.

## Cutting with open blades

Scissors and craft knives are part of every lesson. Treat them like any open blade:

- Good light; look **down from above** so you see the cut line and both hands.
- One hand guides/holds the material; the other operates the tool.
- Sharp tools cut cleaner with less force — less chance of slipping into a finger.
- Controlled cuts only. Do not cut toward a hand you cannot see.

## SDS highlights (real manufacturer sheets)

These are **highlights for makers**, not a substitute for reading the sheet that ships with *your* product. Formulations and revision dates differ — check the SDS for the exact can you buy.

### Heptane / Bestine-type thinner

Cited: [Speedball Bestine Solvent & Thinner SDS](https://www.speedballart.com/content/resources/BT%20Bestine%20SDS.pdf) (n-heptane; also covers Best-Test rubber cement thinner branding) and [CITGO Heptane (19043) SDS](https://www.docs.citgo.com/msds_pi/19043.pdf).

| Figure | Typical SDS value | Plain language |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Flash point | **−9 °C** (~16 °F) (Bestine); CITGO closed-cup **−9 °C** (15.8 °F) | Vapor can ignite far below room temperature. Room air is already “hot” relative to flash point — keep flames, sparks, and pilot lights away. |
| Boiling range | Bestine **88–105 °C** (~190–221 °F) | It evaporates readily at desk temperature; that is why thin films dry and why open volume matters. (Some older contact-cement sheets list boiling near **170–180 °F** — same idea: solvent wants to leave the liquid.) |
| Vapor density | CITGO **~3** (air = 1) | Roughly three times denser than air → sinks and pools low. |
| Signal / core hazards | Danger: highly flammable; aspiration hazard if swallowed; skin irritation; may cause drowsiness/dizziness | Do not drink; do not induce vomiting if swallowed — get poison control / medical help. Ventilate; limit inhalation; gloves help with skin. |

**What to do (SDS posture, short):** adequate ventilation / cross-breeze; no smoking; keep containers closed; absorb small spills with non-combustible absorbent; move to fresh air if you feel lightheaded.

### Rubber cement / contact cement (example)

Cited: [DAP Weldwood Original Contact Cement SDS](https://www.dap.com/media/18168/3050301english.pdf) (flash point **−6 °C**; vapors can flow along surfaces to a distant ignition source and flash back). Your latex cement brand may differ — use **that** product’s SDS — but expect the same class of solvent hazards when the can lists heptane or similar aliphatics.

## Series 1 posture (desk-scale)

1. Open windows / start air moving **before** opening glue or decanted thinner.
2. Decant half-a-soda-can scale (or less), then cap the main thinner.
3. Small glue batches; less open surface during waits.
4. Remember floor pooling — pull air from the **bottom** of the room when you can.
5. Fresh-air break if a session runs long.

Long multi-panel glue days need more than this checklist. See [4 — Heptane safety](/technique/4-heptane-safety).
